Three rounds. Each round has a 48-hour writing window and a new prompt assignment. Only ~0.29% of writers reach the prize tier (10 winners out of 3,456 entrants). Past years' rules confirm round 2 swaps the action prompt for a location prompt, and the final round opens the genre.

Economics

The challenge is funded by the writers. NYC Midnight takes ~$45 per entry. With 3,456 writers, that's ~$155K in fees collected. Roughly 8% goes back out as prize money, 2% goes to charity (writer's choice), and the rest funds judges, judging-feedback time, software, and the company itself.

All 86 groups, graded by Competitive Density Score (CDS) — how many traceable threats each group has, weighted by tier.

CDS scoring rubric

+10per prior 500FC finalist (2023–2025 top-10 / HM) returning in 2026
+5per NYCM vet (prior ranking in any other NYCM challenge)
+3per traditionally-published author (named press, multiple titles)
+2per indie / self-published author (Amazon, Goodreads)
+1per short-form publisher (lit-mag bylines, contest wins)
0hobbyists, unclear names, no findings (most of the field)

Grade bands

SBrutal · 30+ CDS (multiple finalists or stacked pub credits)
ATough · 20–29 CDS (1 finalist + heavy pub presence, or no finalist + very deep field)
BModerate · 10–19 CDS (a few credentialed writers, no dominant figure)
CLight · 5–9 CDS (a couple indie/short pubs)
DSoft · 0–4 CDS (no traceable threats)
Density caveat: All groups have ~40 writers competing for 10 advancing slots. A higher CDS means more credentialed writers fighting for the same 10 slots — a steeper effective bar.
